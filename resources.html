Briefs for Chinese Folk Music

Chinese music can be roughly divided into four types: court music, Literati music, religious music, and folk music. Folk music is crucial in shaping and developing Chinese culture, especially modern revolutionary culture.

For example, folk music was the ancestor of Chinese poetry and literature since Pre-Qin. Even in modern Chinese society, folk music still plays a role in recording people’s daily lives.

There is a clear difference between Chinese and Western music. Unlike Western Music, which focuses on the vertical structure of music, Chinese music focuses on the development of melody.
